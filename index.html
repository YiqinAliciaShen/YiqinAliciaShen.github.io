Gender Double Standard of Aging

How people perceive aging, or even age itself, differently in men and women.

Susan Sontag once remarked in the essay "Gender Double Standard of Aging", while aging is part of destiny for a man, for a woman, aging is not only her destiny, it is also her vulnerability. This line of research directly investigate how people perceive age and aging in men and women using emperical methods. In collboration with Prof. Yuichi Shoda at UW, we found that women are perceived to be older when all facial aging signs are equal. We also found that being perceived to be older is more negatively correlated with outcomes such as voting intention for women compared to men.

  • ⋅ Shen, Y.A., Shoda, Y. (2020) How Candidates Age and Gender Predict Voter Preference in a Hypothetical Election. Psychological Science
  • ⋅ Shen, Y.A., Shoda, Y. (Working Paper) The Gender Double Standard of Age Perception: The Effect of Perceived Gender on Facial Age Estimation

Measurement of Implicit Bias

Understand and improve the Implicit Association Test (IAT) and brief Implicit Association Test (BIAT) to measure individual preferences that are outside of one's awareness.

The implicit association test is one of the most widely used measurements of implicit (outside of awareness) preferences in both social psychology and the real world. It is often used in the context of measuring attitudes that the individuals are not aware of, or ones in which the indivdiual would choose not to self-report. Examples of those attitudes includes racial and gender preferences. This line of research investigates technical aspects of the IAT and BIAT and how that influences various types of validity, and establish boundaries for its future applications.

Persistant Under-representation of Women's Science in High Profile Journals

Using data science technique, we showed that under-representation of women in high impact factor journals has persisted over more than a decade, with glacial improvement over time.

We collected research article publication records from 15 high-profile multidisciplinary and neuroscience journals for 2005-2017 and analyzed the representation of women over time, as well as its relationship with journal impact factor. We find that women authors have been persistently underrepresented in high-profile journals. This under-representation has persisted over more than a decade, with glacial improvement over time. Even within our limited group of high profile journals, the percent of female first and last authors is negatively associated with journal impact factor. Since publishing in high-profile journals is a gateway to academic success, this underrepresentation of women may contribute to the lack of women at the top of the academic ladder.

  • References:
  • ⋅ Shen, Y. A., Shoda, Y., & Fine, I. (2018). Too few women authors on research papers in leading journals. Nature, 555(7695).
  • ⋅ Shen, Y. A., Webster, J. M., Shoda, Y., & Fine, I. (2018). Persistent underrepresentation of women’s science in high profile journals. BioRxiv, 275362.

Seattle ORCA Transit Card Data Cleaning and Visualization

Helped WSDOT clean and visualize transit data

As part of UW's data science for social good project, our team helped city of Seattle clean and visualize various aspects of the One Regional Card for All (ORCA) data. Combining 4 different data sources, our team cleaned the build the database, then built dashboard to dynamically visualize ridership information, neighbourhood level usage, and bus overload information.
